Mukundhan Srinivasan

Engineer at NVIDIA^2. IEEE Volunteer, Ex-Paytm Labs. Organiser DLBLR.
Fedrer Fan. Enjoy entroy. Information merchant.

I’m back at NVIDIA in TDot, Canada for a second innings as a Senior Data Scientist focusing on ML at datacenter scale. Broadly, I’m interested in machine learning mechanisms and related system engineering. Research topics that I follow are generation applied to language, dialogue systems specifically generation & evlauation, and lastly ideas on generalization. I’m often facinated by language.

I had a brief stint as Lead ML Architect at Paytm Labs, Canada. Prior that, I was a Senior Architect at NVIDIA in Bangalore where I had the opportunity to collaborate with researchers/engineers while building deep models, platforms, pipelines for training and inferencing workloads on GPUs.

A long time ago, I was a co-founder of Market Learning Labs where we built developer tools for computer vision and NLP applications that ultimately failed due to non-functional GTM/sales. I was fortunate to start my career as a Research Assistant at the Indian Institute of Science, Bangalore working on Indic language OCR and TTS systems.

I’m an active IEEE volunteer contributing back to the scientific community for about a decade now. Presently, I serve as the Vice-Chair for the Membership Development Committee at the IEEE Region 7 (Canada). Recently, I was promoted to a Senior IEEE Member and graduated from the VoLT program. Reach out if you are interested in building communities! From time to time, I’ve advised select startups in the deeptech space.

Outside of CS/ML, I have a keen interest in studying the structure of economies, history of societies, and south-Indian classical music. I’m a student/practitioner of Sanatana dharma. I spend a disproportionate amount of time on horology and writing instruments.

I stopped following cricket after Rahul Dravid retired except for that occasional game. I’m a huge Federer fan - needless to say, no one has a better inside-out forehand. I continue to learn and play chess (mainly because it is another tab in the browser). I almost always play e4-e5.

I used to live in (beautiful) authentic part of Bangalore closer to CTR’s benne dose and Veena store’s idli chutney before moving to Toronto. Being from the south, I love my filter kaapi (yes, Kaapi!) and can be brided with a good one.

Research Colaborators: Prof. Mitesh Khapara and Prof. Balaraman Ravindran, IIT Madras; Prof. Venkatesh Babu, IISc Bangalore; Prof. Vinay P Namboodiri, IIT Kanpur; Prof. Debdoot Sheet, IIT Kharagpur; Dr. Tavpritesh Sethi, Stanford Medical; Prof. Anurag Agrawal, IGIB; Dr. Vidur Mahajan, CARING Research
